Effective Study Techniques: A practical guide to getting more from your learning time.

### Notes:

Welcome. This presentation covers five evidence-based study techniques that help you retain information more effectively and make better use of limited study time.

<!-- Slide number: 2 -->

Active Recall: Testing yourself is more effective than re-reading.

### Notes:

Instead of passively reviewing notes, close them and try to recall the material from memory. This forces your brain to retrieve information, which strengthens long-term retention far more than highlighting or re-reading.

<!-- Slide number: 3 -->

Spaced Repetition: Review material at increasing intervals over time.

### Notes:

Rather than cramming, revisit content after one day, then three days, then a week. Each review session reinforces the memory trace and pushes the next review further into the future, making study sessions progressively shorter.

<!-- Slide number: 4 -->

Audio Learning: Convert your materials to audio and study on the go.

### Notes:

Text-to-speech tools allow you to turn lecture notes, presentations, and readings into audio files. This enables you to engage with course material during commutes, exercise, or other activities where reading is not practicable.

<!-- Slide number: 5 -->

Summary: Combine methods for the best results.

### Notes:

No single technique works in isolation. Active recall surfaces gaps, spaced repetition locks in what you know, and audio learning extends the time available for study. Used together, they significantly increase the efficiency of your study sessions.
